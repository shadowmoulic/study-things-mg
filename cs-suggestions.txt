Model Question Bank
Module 1: Introduction to Algorithm
Objective Questions (1 Mark Each)
1. What is a flowchart?
2. Name any two high-level programming languages.
3. What is an algorithm?
4. What is pseudocode?
Short Answer Questions (5 Marks Each)
1. Define algorithm and flowchart. Explain any four characteristics of a good algorithm
with suitable examples from simple everyday problems (such as making tea or adding
two numbers).
2. Write an algorithm and draw a flowchart to find the largest of three numbers entered
by the user. Clearly show all decision steps and the complete flow of control.
3. Explain the different types of symbols used in a flowchart (terminal, process, decision,
input/output, connector). For each symbol, describe its purpose and give a small
example of how it is used.
4. Write an algorithm and draw a flowchart to calculate the factorial of a given positive
integer n. Your flowchart should clearly show initialization, loop condition, update,
and termination.
5. Consider the problem: “Read the marks of a student in three subjects, compute the
total and average, and then display whether the student has passed or failed assuming
pass if average ≥ 40.”
(a) Write a step-by-step algorithm.
(b) Draw the corresponding flowchart.
6. Discuss any five differences between algorithms and flowcharts in terms of
representation, readability, ease of modification, debugging, and implementation.
7. Write an algorithm and draw a flowchart to sum all even numbers between 1 and n
(including n if it is even). Clearly indicate loop initialization, loop condition, and
increment.
8. Given the problem: “Accept an integer and check whether it is a palindrome,”
(a) Rewrite it as a proper algorithm using numbered steps.
(b) Draw a flowchart for the same problem, showing how the number is reversed and
compared.
9. Explain the role of sequence, selection, and iteration in algorithms. For each control
structure, describe the concept and sketch a small fragment of a flowchart that
illustrates it.
10. Write an algorithm and draw a flowchart to read the salaries of 10 employees and
count how many earn more than 50,000. Show clearly how you will control the
repetition and update the count.
