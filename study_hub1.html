Theoretical Frameworks & Labour

⚡ Feminist Strands

  • Liberal: Reform within systems. Focus on education & legal rights.
  • Marxist: Class structures as the root. Property breeds oppression.
  • Radical: Patriarchy as a universal system of control over bodies.
  • Postmodern: Challenges fixed identities and "monolithic" narratives.
  • Ecofeminism: Link between exploitation of women and nature.

🏢 Public vs Private Binary

  • Public: State, Economy, Law. Defined by Rationality/Egoism.
  • Private: Family, Household. Defined by Emotions/Loyalty.
  • Triple Burden: Productive (work), Reproductive, and Domestic labour.
  • Sexual Politics: Kate Millett's view of power in the most intimate spaces.

💰 Economic Disparities

  • Occupational Segregation: Horizontal (types of jobs) vs Vertical (rank/hierarchy).
  • Invisible Labour: Unpaid household chores omitted from national income.
  • Technology Effect: Mechanization often replaces female husking/manual labor.

🧬 Kinship Models

  • Patrilineal: Identity mapped via father. Daughters as "transient" members.
  • Matrilineal: Identity via mother. (Nayar, Khasi, Garo tribes).
  • Bilateral: Equally through both parents (Common in SE Asia).

Understanding Patriarchy

DEFINITION

⚖️ Rule of the Father

  • Etymology: Power of the 'patriarch' over women, slaves, and juniors.
  • Ideology: Believes men are superior and women are "man's property."
  • Control Areas: Labour power, Reproduction, Sexuality, Mobility, and Property.
ORIGINS

⏳ The Great Shift

  • Egalitarian Origins: Tribes originally shared resources; "Mother Right" was common.
  • Agricultural Surplus: Private property led men to seek inheritance rights.
  • Institutionalization: Shift from equitable tribes to male-commanded hierarchies.
IDEOLOGY & IMPACT

🧠 Psychological Patriarchy

  • Kamla Bhasin: Systemic oppression in both private & public spheres.
  • bell hooks: Interlocking systems of "Imperialist White-Supremacist Capitalist Patriarchy."
  • Repression: Boys "normally traumatized" by repressing emotions for strength.
ALTERNATIVES

🌱 Feminist Manhood

  • Partnership Style: Based on interbeing & interdependency, not domination.
  • Integrity: Replacing "power over" with responsibility for self and others.
  • Non-violence: Commitment to peace and mutuality as the core of identity.
